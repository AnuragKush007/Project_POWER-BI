# D-Mart Sales Analysis Dashboard

## Overview

This project analyzes retail sales data using Power BI to evaluate revenue growth, profitability, customer segments, product performance, and overall business trends.

An interactive multi-page Power BI dashboard was developed to help business stakeholders monitor key performance indicators, analyze year-over-year growth, identify high-performing products and customer segments, and support data-driven sales and inventory decisions.

## Business Problem

A retail business such as D-Mart generates large volumes of sales data across products, categories, customers, and time periods. Without a centralized analytical dashboard, it can be difficult for decision-makers to monitor business performance, identify revenue and profit drivers, track growth trends, and determine which products and customer segments contribute the most value.

The goal of this project was to transform raw sales data into an interactive Power BI dashboard that provides clear visibility into sales performance and supports informed business decision-making.

## Data Cleaning and Preparation

The dataset was cleaned and transformed before dashboard development.

Key preparation steps included:

- Reviewed the dataset structure and column data types.
- Checked the data for missing and inconsistent values.
- Standardized relevant fields for accurate reporting.
- Transformed raw data using Power Query.
- Prepared sales, profit, customer, product, and date-related fields for analysis.
- Created a structured data model for efficient dashboard reporting.
- Established relationships between relevant tables.
- Created DAX measures for sales, profitability, growth, and cumulative performance analysis.

## Key Insights

- Sales performance varies significantly across products and customer segments, highlighting clear differences in revenue contribution.
- A small group of top-performing products contributes a substantial share of overall sales, making them important for inventory and promotional planning.
- Year-over-Year analysis helps identify periods of business growth and performance changes over time.
- Customer segmentation reveals differences in purchasing contribution across customer groups.
- Revenue and profit analysis shows that high sales volume does not always result in equally high profitability.
- Product-level analysis helps identify high-value products as well as areas requiring pricing, promotion, or inventory review.
- Cumulative sales analysis provides a clear view of long-term business growth and sales progression.

## Business Recommendations

- Prioritize inventory availability for consistently high-performing products.
- Use product-level sales and profitability analysis to improve inventory allocation decisions.
- Focus marketing campaigns on valuable customer segments with strong sales contribution.
- Review products with high sales but comparatively low profit margins to identify pricing or cost optimization opportunities.
- Use Year-over-Year growth trends to support future sales forecasting and performance planning.
- Monitor cumulative sales trends regularly to evaluate progress toward long-term business targets.
- Apply targeted promotions to underperforming products and categories based on sales and profitability analysis.

4. Use the available slicers and filters to explore the data.
5. Analyze KPI cards to monitor overall business performance.
6. Review sales and revenue trends across different periods.
7. Explore product and customer segment performance.
8. Use the visual insights to evaluate sales growth, profitability, and business opportunities.
